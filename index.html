About QUAYNI

QUAYNI is a community-driven token on the Base network inspired by Latin culture, humor, and positive energy. The project promotes creativity, financial inclusion, and social impact through collective effort and transparency.

QUAYNI aims to support social assistance initiatives, helping vulnerable communities and contributing to charitable causes through community-led actions and donations.

Social Impact · Friaje in the Andes

In the Andes, intense cold (“friaje”) is not just “bad weather” – it is a yearly emergency that silently affects families, children, elders and the animals they depend on.

En los Andes, cada año ocurre un fenómeno devastador: el friaje intenso.

Temperaturas extremas bajo cero, vientos helados, nieve, y noches largas que golpean sin piedad a:

  • los pobladores de las alturas,
  • niños y ancianos vulnerables,
  • y a los auquenidos: llamas, alpacas y vicuñas.

El friaje no es un simple "frío". Es una emergencia silenciosa que cada año provoca:

  • pérdida de animales,
  • enfermedades en familias,
  • hambruna,
  • falta de mantas, alimento y refugio,
  • animales delgados, cansados, débiles,
  • niños temblando bajo techos precarios.

En medio de esta realidad surge QUAYNI: un token que busca canalizar recursos, visibilidad y apoyo hacia quienes viven esta situación cada invierno, conectando la tecnología con la solidaridad real.

Roadmap

Phase 1 · Foundation

Token deployment on Base, contract verification, renounce ownership, initial community building, and core narrative around friaje and Andean support.

Phase 2 · Social Visibility

Website launch, presence on social networks, partnerships with local initiatives, documentation of needs in Andean regions and transparent communication.

Phase 3 · Donations & Support

Organize community-driven campaigns to provide food, blankets and basic supplies for families and animals affected by intense cold in the Andes.

Phase 4 · Expansion

Strengthen the ecosystem, expand the community, and create tools for easier contributions, reports and long-term social impact projects.
